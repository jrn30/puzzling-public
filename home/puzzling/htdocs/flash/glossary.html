Glossary

Green: Nouns. The nouns in the list are followed by the articles le, la or les to inform you of their gender.
Blue:Adverbs, they enhance the meaning of the verbs
Red: Verbs: doing words. In brackets, after the verb you will find pronouns such as "ils". This is to show you who is the person doing that action. Also in red are the Adjectives: Describing words
Infinitive: It is the verb by itself, eg: to eat. In French you often needto know the infinitive of a verb to help you guess it in different tenses.

appelèrent (ils) :
verb, simple past, called.Infinitive: appeler

à cause de :
adverb, because of or thanks to

a été (elle) :
verb, present perfect, has been. It can also mean was.

a été construite (elle) :
verb, past tense, passive form was built. The past participle ends in e as it agrees with the object that was built: la ville

afin de :
conjunction, in order to.

aide moi :
imperative, help me! The more polite form is "Aidez moi"!

ail (l') :
noun, masculine, garlic. French cuisine requires a lot of "ail"!

allait devenir (elle) :
verb, was about to become.Infinitive: devenir.

anneau (l') :
noun, masculine, ring. The Lord of the Rings is translated as "Le Seigneur des Anneaux"!

apparaitre (elle) :
verb, future, will appear. Infinitive: apparaître.

apprendre :
verb transitive: to learn something. Or you can say apprendre à quelqu'un to teach someone.

après :
adverb, after. Opposite of avant, which means before.

argent (l') :
colour, silver.The adjective is argenté. You can also add an e for feminine and a s for plural.

assiégée :
verb, past participle used as an adjective, besieged.Infinitive: assiéger.Here an e is added to the past participle,as it agrees with "La cité", which is feminine.

au bord du :
On the bank of (here followed by a masuline noun: le Rhin). Au bord de la (if followed by a feminine),au bord des (if followed by a plural)

av.J-C. :
abbreviation: avant Jésus Christ (B.C.)

baie (la) :
noun, feminine, bay.

bâtirent (ils) :
verb, historical tense, built. Infinitive : batir

beurre (le) :
noun, masculine, butter

bosquet (le) :
noun, masculine, bush.

bourg (le) :
noun, masculine, village. You can see this word in Strasbourg, the village of Strasse (Streets in German).

brûlée :
past participle used as an adjective, burnt. Here an e is added as it agrees with "La cité", which is feminine.

caisse (la) :
noun, feminine, till."Emmène les articles à la caisse", bring the items to the till.

carrière (la) :
noun, feminine, carry

ceux :
pronoun, plural: those. Its singular form is "celui" (masculine) or "celle" ( feminine).

chaudron (le) :
noun, masculine, cauldron.

chaussée (la) :
noun, feminine, pavement. The s at the end here refers to the plural form: les chaussées.

chaussettes (les) :
noun, feminine, socks. It is often used in its plural form.

chef lieu (le) :
noun, masculine, county town.

Choisissez :
Verb, imperative, choose. A verb by itself ending in ez is often a suggestion made in a polite manner.

clef :
noun, feminine, key. This can also be spellt "la clé".

combien de fois :
how many times

commencer :
verb, infinitive, to start

connue :
past participle used as an adjective, known. Infinitive: connaître. An e has been added as La Cité is a feminine noun, which the adjective must agree with.

connut (elle) :
verb, simple past, knew. Infinitive: connaître

conquise :
past participle used as an adjective, conquered.Here an e is added as it agrees with "La cité", which is feminine.

continue (elle) :
verb, present, carries on. Infinitive: continuer. On continue à célébrer: One carries on celebrating.

correspondants :
adjective, matching, corresponding. It ends in s here as it refers to "Les ingrédients".

couverture (la) :
noun, feminine, blanket.

couvre (elle) :
verb, present tense, covers. Infinitive: couvrir.

De nos jours :
Adverb, nowadays. Used at the beginning or the end of a sentence.

De tout temps :
Very common expression used at the beginning of a sentence, always.

découvrir :
verb, to return.

délivrèrent (ils) :
verb, historical past, freed. Infinitive: délivrer.

dérivé :
past participle used as an adjective, derived, taken from.

deviendra (il) :
verb, future, will become

devint (elle) :
verb, simple past, became. Infinitive: devenir.

devint (elle) :
verb, simple past, became. Infinitive: devenir.

devise (la) :
noun, feminine, motto.

dévouement (le) :
noun, masculine, devotion.

difficile :
adjective, hard. Opposite of "facile", easy.

donne (il) :
verb, present, gives. Infinitive: donner

dormir :
verb, infinitive, to sleep.

doté :
verb, past participle used as an adjective: equipped with (de). Infinitive: doter

doucement :
adverb, slowly. It is the opposite of "vite", fast

droit (le) :
noun, masculine, duty, tax. It also means a right.

Durant, Pendant :
Adverbs, during. To remember it, think about its first letter, the same as in During!

église (l') :
noun, feminine, church. Une église aux toits arrondis: a church with rounded roofs.

emblème (l') :
noun, masculine, crest.

emplacement (le) :
noun, maculine, site

en bas :
expression, at the bottom

en haut :
expression, at the top.

entend (il) :
verb,present, hears. Infinitive: entendre

entrait (il) :
verb, past continuous tense, came into. Infinitive: entrer dans.

envahirent (ils) :
verb, simple past, invaded.Infinitive: envahir

erreur (l') :
noun, feminine,mistake

espace vide :
masculine, empty space

est rattachée à :
verb, present, passive form: is attached to. The past participle ends in a e here as it agrees with la ville, a feminine noun.

est située :
verb, present tense, passive form (e added at the end as the word here is feminine), is located. Infinitive: se situer. You can also say "elle se situe", which can mean also mean is located or finds itself.

est toujours d'actualité :
Formal expression meaning is still in use.

était (c'etait) :
verb, past tense, was. Often used in the expression: C'était: It was.

étoffe (l') :
noun, feminine, material, fabric.

être défendu :
past participle: to be protected.In French the verb can: pouvoir is followed by the verb in its infinitive form. Here the whole form "pouvait être défendu" therefore means could be protected.

existent (ils) :
verb, present tense, exist. Infinitive: exister.

fabrication (la) :
noun, feminine: making, production of something.

facile :
adjective, easy. Its opposite is difficile: difficult.

faire des paires :
imperative form, match the pairs

fief (le) :
noun, masculine, fief, stronghold

finissez (vous) :
verb, present, finish

fit (elle) :
verb, past tense, did. Infinitive: faire. The verb "faire": to do, is a very irregular verb.

fleuri :
past participle used as an adjective, flowery.

fois (la) :
noun, feminine, time. Trois fois: three times.

fondée :
past participle used as an adjective, founded. It ends in e as it refers to "la ville", feminine.

fondèrent :
verb, simple past, founded. Infinitive: fonder

fortifièrent (ils) :
verb, simple past: built. Infinitive: construire.

frontière (la) :
noun, feminine, border

fut (elle) :
verb, simple past, was. More formal than était, which is an other way of saying was. Infinitive: être.One common plural form is Ils, Elles furent: They were.

fut libérée (elle) :
verb, simple past, passive form: it was freed. Infinitive: Libérer quelquechose, quelqu'un: to free something, someone.

gagnerez :
verb, future, will win

grâce à :
conjunction, thanks to

grec, grecque :
adjective, Greek. The feminine form takes "que" at the end of grec (the masculine form).

guéri :
past participle used as an adjective, cured. It ends with an e when it refers to "elle", feminine.

héritier (l') :
noun, masculine, heir. The feminine form is "héritière", heiress.

il fait beau :
expression, the weather is nice.

Il faut :
Expression found at the beginning of a sentence to say you have to...

il n'est pas surprenant que :
It is not surprising that

Il te reste :
Common expression meaning: you are left with…..

il vous faudra :
expression, future tense, you will need

intégra (il) :
verb,simple past, integrated. Infinitive: intégrer.

iront (ils) :
verb, future, will go. Infinitive: aller

jeu (le) :
noun, masculine, game. The verb is jouer: to play

lacher :
verb, infinitive, to drop, to let go of.

laine (la) :
noun, feminine, wool. A woollen jumper: un pull de laine.

libre :
adjective, free.

lunettes (les) :
noun, feminine, plural, glasses. Mets tes lunettes, wear your glasses

magasin (le) :
noun, masculine, shop. "Faire les magasins": To go shopping

mal :
adjective, bad or adverb, badly. Je vois mal, I see badly, I can hardly see.

marin (le) :
noun, masculine, sailor. The end at the end here is added to make the plural. Un marin, deux marins.

mer (la) :
noun, feminine, the sea

métier (le) :
noun, masculine, job.

mieux :
adjective, superlative form, le mieux: the best. It can also mean better, when comparing two items.

montre (la) :
noun, feminine, watch. It also means, show! As in Show me: Montre moi!

mot (le) :
noun, masculine, word.

On ne peut plus :
Expressions, negative form: One can't any more.

On pense :
Present, one thinks. On is often use in French to refer to a group of people or an action done by most people.

ont été trouvés (ils) :
Past, passive form, were found.The past participle (trouvés) agrees with the object (Des sites préhistoriques). This is why it ends in s.

palmier (le) :
noun, masculine, palm tree. They are very common in Nice.

parapluie (le) :
noun, masculine, umbrella. "Pluie" in French means rain, it makes it easier to remember the word parapluie.

parc d'attractions (le) :
noun, masculine, leisure parc. One of the most famous ones in France is "Le Parc Astérix".

perdre :
verb, to lose. Sans perdre, without losing.

permis (le) :
noun, masculine, driving licence.

plaisantes :
adjective, pleasing, agreeable. Here it ends in es as it refers to "les villes", feminine and plural.

pompier (le) :
noun, masculine, fireman. The plural simply use the same word with an s in the end.

pont (le) :
noun, masculine, bridge.

porte-monnaie (le) :
noun, masculine, purse. Literally, this word means a change carrier!

pourrez (vous) :
verb, future, will be able to. Infinitive: pouvoir

pourrons (nous) :
verb, future, will be able to. Nous pourrons partir: we will be able to leave

poussin :
noun, masculine, chick."Jaune comme un poussin", yellow as a chick is a common French saying.

pouvait (il) :
verb, past, could. Infinitive: pouvoir.

présenter :
verb, infinitive, to show, to present

prêt :
adjective, ready.

querelle :
noun, feminine, quarrel.

qui :
pronoun, that, which. It can also mean who or whom.

recette (la) :
noun, feminine, recipe. You can also say "La recette de cuisine".

redevint (elle) :
verb, simple past, became again. Infinitive: redevenir. The prefix RE in front of a verb means again.

relier :
verb, infinitive: to link. Relier les problèmes aux solutions: Link the problems to the solutions

remontent (elles) :
verb, present, date from.

renaît (elle) :
verb, present, revives, is reborn. Infinitive: renaître.

retournées :
past participle,used as an adjective upside down.The es at the end refers to "les cartes", feminine and plural.

retourner :
verb, to go back. All verbs starting with re in French mean that the action is being done again or that something goes back to its previous state.

route (la) :
noun, feminine, road

rue (la) :
noun, feminine, street.

sais (tu) :
verb, present, know, can. Tu sais? Can you?

sans :
preposition, without

se rencontrent (elles) :
reflexive verb, present, meet each other. Infinitive: se rencontrer.

se révolta (elle) :
reflexive verb, past tense, rebelled against (contre). Infinitive: se révolter

se situe (elle) :
reflexive verb, present, is located. The French use this expression which means"finds itself" to speak about the location of a place.

se sont installés (ils) :
Reflexive verbs,simple past, they got settled. In French a reflexive verb uses être to form the past tense.

sel et poivre :
noun, masculine, salt and pepper.

serez (vous) :
verb, future: will be. Infinitive: être

s''etend :
reflexive verb, present, stretches out. Infinitive: s''etendre.

si :
conjunction, if

siège (le) :
noun, masculine, seat, Head Office. In French, the word refers to the object as well as to a building.

signifie :
verb, present, means. Infinitive: signifier

s'installèrent (ils) :
reflexive verb, simple past, got settled.

sol (le) :
noun, masculine, floor. Le sol glisse, the floor is slippery.

sourd :
adjective, deaf. Its feminine form is sourde.

teinture (la) :
noun, feminine, dyeing of something.

tient (elle) :
verb, present tense, takes. The whole expression, in its infinitive form is "tenir son nom de" to take one's name from..

tous :
adjective, every. It can also mean all.

tout au long de :
adverb, throughout. The adjective "long" gives an idea of length in time or in space.

troupe (la) :
noun, feminine, company, military group.

trouverez (vous) :
verb, future, will find.Infinitive: trouver.

usine (l') :
noun, feminine, factory.

veste (la) :
noun, feminine, jacket.

vêtements (les) :
noun, masculine, plural, clothes.

veuillez accepter :
please accept, this is a formal expression used when presenting rewards or prices. It can also be found at the end of letters: "veuillez accepter mes sincères salutations": Please receive my best regards.

veux (tu) :
verb, present tense, want. Tu veux?Do you want

voile (la) :
noun, feminine, sail.

voir :
verb, infinitive, to see. On peut voir: One can see

vont (ils) :
verb, present tense, are going. The verb aller: to go, is very often used in French to form the future. Ils vont construire: They are going to build

voulaient (ils) :
verb, past continuous, wanted. Infinitive: vouloir.

In September 2001 the University of Cambridge was allocated three years of funding from the Higher Education Funding Council for England (HEFCE). This was to enable the university to build on its existing strategy for raising aspirations, and widening participation and access, by further developing and strengthening its relationships with schools, sixth-form colleges and further education institutions involved in the Excellence in Cities (EiC), Education Action Zones (EAZ) and AimHigher programmes.

Initial funding was also provided for a new and ambitious initiative: the provision of web-based outreach for use by students and teachers in schools and colleges. In total, 11 projects were funded; six of which are wholly web-based.

In February 2002, the university's Centre for Applied Research in Educational Technologies (CARET) was one of eight departments awarded aspiration-raising funds to assist the University of Cambridge to deliver its aspiration-raising agenda as part of its widening participation and access programme by July 2004.

Human Factory is one of four projects being developed at CARET as part of the University’s aspiration-raising scheme. The other three projects are:
Brainteasers and Puzzles (cross-curricular thinking skills)
rEvolution (natural sciences)
Converse (English Literature)

The purpose of all the aspiration-raising projects is to encourage and inspire 11-19 year olds - in particular, those with low or no aspirations and without a background or tradition in further or higher education - to consider studying at a further and/or higher education level as a worthwhile investment in their future.

The resources being developed are designed to complement the national curriculum, and challenge and engage the target groups in a learning process that raises their achievements.

Human Factory is a free 3D game about the human digestive system. Users are taken on a journey through the digestive tract and encouraged to interact with their surroundings in order to earn points.

Every object within the game has factual information written about it and at the end of each section (mouth, stomach, duodenum and ileum) learners are tested on their knowledge of the biological, chemical and physical processes associated with digestion. Further points may be collected by taking photos of different objects within the environment.

As the user travels from the mouth to the colon, s/he is accompanied by Mr Sweetcorn who has a wealth of bizarre factoids to reveal. He also acts as Quiz Master.

The Human Factory game may be used in the classroom to teach National Curriculum content in Science and ICT for Key Stages 2, 3 and 4. Due to its flexible nature it can be used on various levels to suit class timescales e.g. the Body Map could be used for a 10 minute lesson on the location of the digestive organs, or the macromolecular scenes could be used for a 40 minute lesson on enzymes.

The accompanying website contains careers information for young people inspired by the game and considering a career in a field relating to Human Biology or Medicine.

Further information on the project can be seen in the About section of the website.

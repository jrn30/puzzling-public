There are all kinds of resources out there to help you choose your career. It's worth thinking about what you might like to do as early as possible because you may need to study particular subjects at GCSE or A-Level in order to get onto the right Higher Education course.

Two good careers resources are Aim Higher and Connexions. They offer advice on a wide range of issues, including careers and course advice. Connexions have offices around Britain that provide free one to one careers advice and a comprehensive library of careers leaflets detailing course requirements, salary, hours of work etc. The UCAS website is a good place to look for full-time undergraduate courses in the United Kingdom and the NHS careers site has information on the type of careers available in the NHS and specific courses.

We've selected the following jobs as examples of what you could do in fields relating to Medicine or Human Biology. The listed websites are suggestion of where you can look further and are not to be taken as an endorsement of any kind.
  • Biochemist
  • Dentist
  • Dietician
  • Forensic Scientist
  • Medical Illustrator
  • Naturopath
  • Speech and Language Therapist


Biochemists study how living things work at the molecular level. It is laboratory based work and could involve areas such as genetics, enzyme study, nutrition and metabolism.

www.biochemistry.org - The Biochemical Society
www.acb.uk.org - The Association of Clinical Biochemists
The Connexions website has detailed information about working as a Biochemist.
Connexions leaflet QOD5 "Careers with Biochemistry"




Dentists treat and prevent dental and oral disease as well as correcting dental malformations and injuries.

www.bda-dentistry.org.uk - The British Dental Association
www.gdc-uk.org - The General Dental Council
The Connexions website has detailed information about working as a Dentist.
Connexions leaflet JAF1 "Careers in Dental health"




Dieticians can work in hospitals, in the community or in the food industry. They translate the science of nutrition into practical information about food.

www.bda.uk.com - The British Dietetic Association
The Connexions website has detailed information about working as a Dietician.
Connexions leaflet JAV1 "Dietetics"




Forensic Scientists work closely with the police to find and check scientific evidence in order to help solve crimes.

www.forensic-science-society.org.uk - The Forensic Science Society
www.forensic.gov.uk - The Forensic Science Service Headquarters
The Connexions website has detailed information about working as a Forensic Scientist.
Connexions leaflet QOT1 "Forensic Science"




Medical illustrators may work as photographers, video producers, artists or graphic designers. They clinically record patients using still or moving pictures or they produce artwork for posters and brochures.

www.imi.org.uk - The website for the Institue of Medical Illustrators has information on specific courses and job vacancies
Connexions leaflet ED3 "Medical Illustration and Photography"




Naturopaths believe that illness is caused by an obstruction to the natural flow of energy in the body. After a detailed study of the patient, naturopaths give advice on diet, herbal remedies, rest and exercise.

www.naturopathy.org.uk - Naturopathic Forum.
The Connexions website has detailed information about working as a Naturopath.
Connexions leaflet JOD3 "Complementary Medicine"
